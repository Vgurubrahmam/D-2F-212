CODING CONTEXT

About the coding context

In the context of coding, an "event" is like a notification that something has happened in a computer program. It's a way for different parts of the program to communicate with each other. Imagine it's like a bell that rings when a specific action occurs, such as a button being clicked on a website. When that button is clicked, an "event" is triggered, and the program can respond to it by running a set of instructions or code. Events are important in coding because they allow programs to be interactive and respond to user actions, making software more dynamic and user-friendly. So, in simple words, an event is like a signal that tells a computer program to do something in response to an action..

START UP

About the start up

A startup event is like a big gathering for new and small businesses that are just getting started. It's a place where entrepreneurs and innovators come together to share their ideas, show off their products or services, and meet potential investors or customers. Think of it as a marketplace of fresh ideas and business opportunities. People at startup events might pitch their business plans, showcase their inventions, or network with others who can help their businesses grow. It's a chance to learn, collaborate, and get support for turning new ideas into successful businesses.
